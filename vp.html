Meet the Running Mate

William “Anubis Shoshanna” Daugherty

Prospective vice-presidential candidate of the Guillotine Party. Born and raised in San Francisco — a youth advocate, a volunteer for public servants, and a frontline health worker with lived experience of homelessness, dedicated to the party's principles and platform.

Roots

A San Franciscan, through and through

William “Anubis Shoshanna” Daugherty was born and raised in San Francisco, and their work has stayed close to the people their city too often overlooks — unhoused young people, those in recovery, and working families navigating a system that wasn't built for them.

That closeness is not abstract. Daugherty has personally experienced homelessness — and that lived experience is the foundation of their advocacy. They know firsthand what it means to be treated as disposable by a system that criminalizes poverty, and they carry that understanding into every part of this work.

Their path runs through grassroots advocacy and frontline care: volunteering for public servants, serving on youth policy boards, and delivering health and recovery services. It's experience grounded not in consultant strategy, but in the daily reality of policy as it lands on real lives — the same conviction that drives the Guillotine Party.

Why they're running

Committed to the movement

William “Anubis Shoshanna” Daugherty stands fully behind the Guillotine Party's principles and platform: constitutional liberty, anti-corruption, economic dignity, accountability, transparency, equal justice, and public service over private gain.

They join this ticket movement-first — to help build durable grassroots power from the ground up, recruit and support candidates, and create a political home for the people the system has stopped representing. The work is organizing, and the goal is to take government back for the people it was meant to serve.

No human being should be treated as disposable. No public office should be treated as private property.
