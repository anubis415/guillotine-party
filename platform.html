Preamble

Our republic stands at a crossroads

We, the people of the United States, recognize that political corruption, corporate influence, and institutional decay have weakened the promise of American democracy. Too many citizens have been asked to accept stagnation, inequality, and unaccountable power as normal. We reject that future.

The Guillotine Party was founded to restore accountability, liberty, and justice. We believe government must answer to the people, not to donors, lobbyists, or entrenched interests. Our platform is a call to renew the republic through transparency, fairness, and decisive reform.

Why the Guillotine

A symbol of accountability — not violence

The guillotine represents the end of privilege without consequence, and the beginning of a system where power can no longer shield itself from the law. It represents:

  • Cutting corruption at its root
  • Ending political privilege
  • Restoring justice
  • Returning power to the people
  • Making public office a duty, not a path to enrichment
  • Ensuring that no leader is above scrutiny

The blade symbolizes decisive reform. The scales symbolize justice. Together, they represent justice through accountability. We choose this symbol because reform must be clear, firm, and impossible to ignore. The people deserve a government that acts with courage, not one that excuses failure and protects the powerful.

Core Principles

The values behind every policy

These principles guide every policy we support. They reflect a simple belief: government should protect rights, serve the public, and remain answerable to the people at all times.

Constitutional Liberty Anti-Corruption Economic Dignity Accountability Transparency Equal Justice Public Service Over Private Gain
Pillar One

Constitutional Rights

Rights belong to the people. They are not granted by politicians, corporations, or bureaucracies — they are inherent, and they must be protected against abuse, erosion, and selective enforcement. We defend:

  • Free speech
  • Privacy rights
  • Due process
  • Equal protection
  • Constitutional liberties
  • The right to assemble and petition
  • The right to fair treatment under the law

We oppose any effort to weaken civil liberties in the name of convenience, fear, or political advantage. A free society depends on strong rights and a government restrained by law.

Pillar Two

The Guillotine Amendment

Corruption must be cut at its root. Public office should never be used as a tool for personal enrichment, insider advantage, or permanent political power. We support:

  • Ending dark money
  • Banning congressional stock trading
  • Term limits
  • Lobbying restrictions
  • Government transparency
  • Strong ethics enforcement
  • Public disclosure of conflicts of interest

The Guillotine Amendment would restore trust by making government cleaner, clearer, and more accountable. It would ensure that those who make the laws are bound by the same standards they impose on everyone else.

Those who make the laws must live under them.
Pillar Three

Economic Rights

Freedom requires dignity. A person cannot fully participate in civic life while struggling to survive. Economic security is not a luxury; it is the foundation of a stable and just society. We support access to:

  • Housing
  • Food
  • Education
  • Healthcare
  • Essential utilities
  • Fair wages
  • Safe working conditions

Every American should have the opportunity to live with safety, stability, and hope. That means building an economy that rewards work, protects families, and prevents exploitation. No one should be denied the basic conditions needed to live a meaningful life — opportunity must not be reserved for the wealthy alone.

Closing Statement

The people vs corruption

The real divide is not left versus right. We stand with the people who work, build, serve, and endure. We stand against the systems that exploit them, silence them, and profit from their frustration.

Our mission is simple: restore accountability, defend liberty, and return government to the people it was meant to serve.
