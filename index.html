Why the guillotine?

The guillotine is not a symbol of violence. It is a symbol of accountability — the end of privilege without consequence, and the beginning of a system where power can no longer shield itself from the law.

✂

Cut corruption at its root

End the backroom deals, special favors, and a political culture that rewards loyalty to the system over loyalty to the country.

⚖

Justice through accountability

The blade symbolizes decisive reform; the scales symbolize justice. No leader is above scrutiny.

🏛

Public office as duty

Public service should mean service — not a path to enrichment, insider advantage, or permanent political power.

✊

Power to the people

We are not here to tear America down. We are here to clean it up and return government to those it was meant to serve.

Core principles

These principles guide every policy we support. They reflect a simple belief: government should protect rights, serve the public, and remain answerable to the people at all times.

📜

Constitutional Liberty

Free speech, privacy, due process, and equal protection belong to the people by right — not by permission. A free society depends on strong rights and a government restrained by law.

✂

Anti-Corruption

End dark money, insider advantage, and backroom deals. Corruption must be cut at its root so public office can never be used as a tool for private enrichment.

🏠

Economic Dignity

Freedom requires dignity. Access to housing, healthcare, education, and fair wages is the foundation of a stable and just society — not a luxury for the few.

⚖

Accountability

No leader is above scrutiny. Those who hold power must answer for their actions and face real consequences for misconduct, abuse, or betrayal of public trust.

🔍

Transparency

Government should be clear and open. Spending, decisions, and conflicts of interest belong in public view — not hidden behind closed doors.

🏛

Equal Justice

The same law for everyone. Justice cannot bend to wealth, status, or political connection. Fair treatment under the law is a right, not a privilege.

🤝

Public Service Over Private Gain

Public office is a duty, not a path to enrichment. Those who serve must put the people's interest ahead of their own portfolios and private networks.

Our platform

A focused agenda built on constitutional rights, an end to corruption, and the economic dignity every American deserves.

📜

Constitutional Rights

We defend free speech, privacy, due process, equal protection, and the right to assemble and petition. Rights belong to the people — they are inherent, not granted by politicians or corporations.

⚖

The Guillotine Amendment

End dark money. Ban congressional stock trading. Impose term limits and lobbying restrictions. Demand transparency, strong ethics enforcement, and public disclosure of conflicts of interest.

🏠

Economic Rights

Access to housing, food, education, healthcare, essential utilities, fair wages, and safe working conditions. Freedom requires dignity — economic security is the foundation of a just society.

Volunteer with us

Volunteers are the backbone of the movement. Every volunteer represents the party — and there's a role for everyone, whether you have an hour a week or want to lead a local team.

🚪

Knock doors

Connect directly with voters where they live. Be polite, be brief, be respectful — your goal is to make a positive impression, not win every conversation.

📞

Phone bank

Reach supporters and persuade undecided voters from anywhere. Keep calls short, clear, and respectful, and thank every person for their time.

📣

Host & staff events

Bring people together, build momentum, and grow the base. Events turn community energy into lasting organizing power.

🤝

Community outreach

Deepen trust in neighborhoods and community spaces. Strong relationships are what keep supporters engaged over time.

💬

Spread the word online

Everything you post reflects on the movement. Share approved, accurate information with discipline and help us reach new people.

⭐

Lead a local team

Recruit volunteers, set goals, and organize your area. Field organizers build the durable, repeatable structure a campaign runs on.

What we ask of every volunteer

Be professional, honest, respectful, disciplined, punctual, and prepared. Never threaten, harass, spread misinformation, or argue aggressively. When in doubt, choose the most respectful and responsible approach — and ask a team leader for guidance.
